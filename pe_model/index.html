Model Overview

The Partial Equilibrium (PE) model estimates the trade impact of tariff changes on Uzbekistan's imports across all HS sectors. Based on the WITS-SMART methodology (World Bank / WTO), it isolates the direct market-level effects of a tariff change, holding all other markets constant.

The model covers the full universe of Uzbekistan's import data at the HS-10 digit level, disaggregated by partner country and trade regime (MFN or FTA).

Trade Creation Formula
TCij = Mij · εd · |Δt/(1 + t)|
M = imports, ε = demand elasticity, Δt = tariff change
Trade Diversion Formula
TDij = MijFTA · εs · |Δt/(1 + t)|
Captures shift from FTA to newly competitive MFN partners
Data Sources

• Uzbekistan customs import data (HS-10, partner country, 2022–2025)
• Applied MFN tariff schedule of Uzbekistan
• CIS Free Trade Agreement schedules
• Import demand elasticities: WITS SMART database — sector-specific per HS Section (ε = 0.38–2.80; Kee, Nicita & Olarreaga, 2008)
• Uzbekistan State Customs Committee

Scenario Definitions

The simulator allows any tariff cut from 5% to 100% applied to the current MFN rate. Pre-defined scenarios follow WTO accession negotiation precedents:

−20%: Moderate liberalisation
−50%: Substantial reduction
−80%: Near-full liberalisation
−100%: Complete removal (MFN = 0)

Welfare Components

Consumer surplus gain: lower prices benefit domestic consumers
Revenue change: reduced tariff collection by government
Net welfare: sum of all effects; positive = net gain to economy
