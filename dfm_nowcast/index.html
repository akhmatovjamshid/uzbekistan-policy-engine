What is the Common Factor?
The DFM extracts a single latent variable — the common factor — that captures shared economic momentum across all 34 monthly indicators simultaneously. Think of it as a real-time pulse of the economy: it rises when most indicators are growing above trend and falls when they are slowing down.

How to read the value: The factor is in standardized units (mean = 0, std ≈ 1 over the sample). A value of −0.068 means conditions are very slightly below the historical average — essentially neutral. Values above +1 signal a strong broad-based expansion; values below −1 signal a broad-based slowdown. The path below shows how this economic momentum has evolved since 2017 — peaks correspond to expansions, the 2020 trough to the COVID shock.
