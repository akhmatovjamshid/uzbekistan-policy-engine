Model Documentation — CGE 1-2-3

Overview

The 1-2-3 model (Devarajan, Go, Lewis, Robinson & Sinko, 1997) is a minimal computable general equilibrium framework designed for developing-country policy analysis. It features 1 country, 2 sectors (exports vs. domestic goods), and 3 commodities (exports E, imports M, domestic good D). The framework captures the essential open-economy macroeconomics in a tractable single-equation system.

The Uzbekistan calibration uses 2021 national accounts data. All variables are normalised so that base-year output X = 1 and the domestic price Pd = 1 (numeraire). The model is closed by a flexible real exchange rate that clears the balance of payments.

Price System

Pd = 1.00      (numeraire — domestic price level fixed)
Pe = we × (1 − te) × Er     (export price in domestic currency)
Pm = wm × (1 + tm) × Er     (import price in domestic currency)

CET Production Frontier (Export Supply)

Producers allocate output between exports and domestic sales according to a Constant Elasticity of Transformation (CET) function. The first-order condition gives the export-to-domestic ratio:

eRat = E/Ds = [(1−bt)/bt × Pe/Pd]^σ_t     (FOC ratio)
Ds = X / (at × [bt × eRat^ρ_t + (1−bt)]^(1/ρ_t))
E = eRat × Ds

Parameters: at = 2.42, bt = 0.82, ρ_t = 2.43, σ_t = 0.70

CES Absorption (Import Demand)

Domestic consumers/firms combine imports and domestic goods according to a Constant Elasticity of Substitution (Armington) aggregator. The FOC gives the import-to-domestic ratio:

mRat = M/Dd = [bq/(1−bq) × Pd/Pm]^σ_q     (Armington ratio)
M = mRat × Ds     (domestic market clearing: Dd = Ds)
Q = aq × [bq × M^(−ρ_q) + (1−bq) × Ds^(−ρ_q)]^(−1/ρ_q)

Parameters: aq = 1.91, bq = 0.32, ρ_q = 0.43, σ_q = 0.70

Balance of Payments Closure

The real exchange rate Er is endogenous and adjusts to clear the balance of payments. We solve for Er by bisection on:

f(Er) = Pm(Er) × M(Er) − Pe(Er) × E(Er) − B − re − ft = 0

Import payments minus export receipts must equal the sum of foreign savings B, remittances re, and grants ft. The bisection searches Er ∈ [0.001, 1000] over 100 iterations.

Derived Macro Aggregates

Pq = (Pm×M + Ds) / Q         (composite good price, Pd=1)
Pt = (Pe×E + Ds) / X         (output price)
Px = Pf × Pt                  (GDP deflator)
TAX = ts×Pq×Q + ty×Px×X + tm×wm×Er×M + te×we×Er×E
Y = Px×X + tr + ft + re       (national income)
Sg = TAX − G − tr             (government budget balance)
Cn = (1−sy) × (Y − TAX)      (private consumption)
S = sy×(Y−TAX) + Sg + B      (total savings)
Z = S                           (savings = investment, Walras law)
TB = Pe×E − Pm×M              (trade balance)

Closure Rules

  • Domestic price numeraire: Pd = 1 throughout. All price changes are expressed relative to the domestic price level.
  • Exchange rate closure: Er is flexible and endogenous — it adjusts to balance the BOP. This is the standard "neoclassical" closure.
  • Savings-Investment: Z = S (investment is savings-driven; no separate investment function).
  • Government budget: Sg = TAX − G − tr (residual — government can run a surplus or deficit).
